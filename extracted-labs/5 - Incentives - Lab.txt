General rules of the 3 games Each player starts with 15 points. In every game, there is a 300/0 chance of having an accident that makes you lose points. On average, an accident costs you 10 points. Your goal: keep as many points as possible by deciding whether to buy insurance (or not). Reminder: Insurance is not free —it also costs points. 4

Remarks The insurance company will go bankrupt if it keeps this price and only people with high losses get insurance. If it only has clients with high losses, the premium should be 4.5 points instead of 3 because this is the fair price for a 300/0 risk of losing 15 points. Let us consider such an insurance contract: For the group with potential loss of 15 points Without insurance contract: Accident (30% chance): 0 points left. No accident (700/0 chance): 15 points. === Page 31 ===
Remarks The "All Risk" contract is attractive only for players facing high losses. The "Mini" contract is attractive only for players facing low losses. With two different contracts, each group naturally chooses the one suited to its risk. This is called a self-selection mechanism: The contract menu makes players reveal their type through their choices. It prevents the problem observed with a single contract, where only high-risk players were insured. 31

Takeaways Key Lessons from the Insurance Games Game 1: With one contract at the average price, only high-risk players buy -5 adverse selection insurer makes a loss.
