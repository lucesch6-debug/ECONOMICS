=== Slide 18 ===
Job
search
model
18
Economists have modeled the job search process.
(See the article assigned for this module.)
The model predicts that job seekers use a “
reservation wage
” to decide whether to accept an offer.
They accept any offer
greater than or equal to
this reservation
wage
.

=== Slide 19 ===
Job
search
model
19
The reservation wage changes during the process:
At the beginning, waiting allows job seekers to learn about the market (learning effect), which can lead to a higher reservation wage.
Later in the process, the learning effect diminishes and the risk of unemployment becomes more salient. A long period of job search may also be stigmatizing. As a result, the reservation wage falls.
The reservation wage is influenced by labor market conditions:
High unemployment lowers the reservation wage.
The presence of unemployment benefits increases it.

=== Slide 20 ===
For employers
20
A high reservation wage among job seekers can make it difficult for employers to fill vacancies at the wages they can afford to pay.

=== Slide 22 ===
Debate: Universal income?
22
A high reservation wage among job seekers can make it difficult for employers to fill vacancies at the wages they are able to pay.
A universal income can protect unemployed individuals against poverty
without necessarily increasing the reservation wage too much
, since people continue to receive the universal income even when they accept a job.
However, financing a universal income may be challenging unless it is accompanied by substantial reforms of the tax and benefit systems.
