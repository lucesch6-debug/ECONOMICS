=== Slide 16 ===
What the theory predicts
16
Good 1 has the lowest storage cost and therefore depreciates the least.
As a result, it is the most attractive good to hold and to use as a medium of exchange.
Type 1
directly wants Good 1 for consumption. If immediate consumption is not possible, holding Good 2 is preferable to ending up with Good 3, which depreciates heavily.
Type 2
wants Good 2 for consumption but prefers to hold Good 1 rather than Good 3 when consumption is not immediate.
Type 3
wants Good 3 for consumption but prefers to store Good 1 rather than Good 2 while waiting to trade.
Because
all types rank Good 1 highest among the goods they do not immediately consume
, the theory predicts that
Good 1 will be accepted more often in trade and will circulate more widely than the others
.
If this pattern emerged in the experiment, then a currency has emerged endogenously!

=== Slide 17 ===
Emergence of money
17
In a barter economy, exchanges are inefficient
because they require a
double coincidence of wants
: it is difficult to find someone who both needs what I have and possesses what I want. When agents accept goods they do not intend to consume immediately, these goods may depreciate before a suitable trading partner is found.
The emergence of a good that serves as a medium of exchange
, and that is easy to store,
reduces transaction costs
,
relaxes the double coincidence constraint,
and allows a higher volume of mutually beneficial exchanges to take place.
