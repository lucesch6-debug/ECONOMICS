What is an experiment in economics? A "survey", a "study" in which participants e.g. students, but not only are asked to make decisions in order to test an hypothesis e.g. "HI: X implies Y" under controlled conditions create a "ceteris paribus" environment keeping everything else constant. 40

=== Page 41 ===
Treatments and randomization Treatments For instance, in our case, Groups A and B, receiving different versions of the questionnaire. Randomization In most experiments, subjects are randomly assigned to the different treatments. 41

=== Page 42 ===
What for? Mainly: To test a causal relationship Hl: X implies Y Difficult to test in real life: inverse causality? Spurious effect? In most cases, we can only observe correlations. Solution: experiments! X = different treatments Subjects are randomly assigned to the various treatments (various levels of X). Y is then measured for each treatment. Neither Y nor a third variable can influence X. 42

Other terms Field experiment / Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) Experiments can be conducted outside a lab. People are often not aware that they participate in the study.
